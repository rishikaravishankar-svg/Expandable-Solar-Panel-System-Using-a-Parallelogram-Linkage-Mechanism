# Expandable Solar Panel System Using a Parallelogram Linkage Mechanism

## Project Overview

This project presents the design and development of an expandable solar panel system that uses a parallelogram linkage mechanism to achieve smooth and synchronized deployment. The mechanism allows the solar panel array to expand into a larger working area while maintaining proper panel alignment and retract into a compact configuration for easy storage and transportation.

The project demonstrates how a simple mechanical linkage can improve the practicality of solar energy systems in applications where installation space is limited or portability is required. A complete CAD model was developed, followed by the fabrication of a functional 3D-printed prototype to validate the deployment mechanism.

---

## Abstract

Solar panels are becoming increasingly important as the demand for clean and renewable energy continues to grow. However, conventional solar panel installations require a large fixed area, making them difficult to use in locations where space is limited or portability is important. This inspired the idea of designing a compact solar panel system that can expand when needed and fold back into a smaller size for storage or transportation.

The project uses a parallelogram linkage mechanism, which allows the panels to deploy smoothly while maintaining their orientation and structural stability throughout the motion. The mechanism provides synchronized movement using a simple mechanical design without requiring complex actuation. A complete CAD model and a functional 3D-printed prototype were developed to evaluate the concept and study the deployment behavior. The design demonstrates how deployable mechanisms can improve the usability of solar energy systems in mobile and space-constrained applications while showcasing the application of mechanism design, CAD modeling, and rapid prototyping.

---

## Motivation

Traditional solar panel installations require a relatively large installation area and are not always suitable for portable or temporary applications. This project explores the use of deployable mechanisms to create a solar panel system that occupies minimal space during storage while providing a larger effective surface area after deployment.

The inspiration for this project came from deployable structures used in engineering applications where compact storage and controlled expansion are essential. A parallelogram linkage was selected because it offers synchronized motion, maintains panel orientation during deployment, and achieves these functions using a simple and reliable mechanical design.

## Why a Parallelogram Linkage?

A parallelogram linkage maintains the relative orientation of connected links while allowing smooth expansion and contraction. This makes it an ideal mechanism for deployable structures because the solar panels remain properly aligned throughout the deployment process without requiring multiple actuators or complex control systems.

The mechanism also distributes motion uniformly across the structure, resulting in a compact, mechanically efficient, and easy-to-manufacture design.
